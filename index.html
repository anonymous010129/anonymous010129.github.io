ConRet Demo page

Overview

ConRet is a retrieval-augmented audio-conditioned generator on both modalities, and the first audio-conditioned model on the text-to-audio side. Earlier AC-V2A systems train with a slice of the ground-truth audio itself as the reference, which creates a train-test gap. ConRet replaces self-reference with retrieval-sampled references and adds a multi-way contrastive forward so the model must produce distinguishable outputs when only the reference changes.

Training Data

Around 2M LLM-augmented audios from 182,614 VGGSound clips × 11 source/context prompts; DeSync-filtered to 679K (114K unique GTs), curated into Pool A (679K GT×Aug) and Pool B (71K Aug×Aug retrieval).

Figure 1

ConRet overview. Top: the three-step VGGSound-ConRet dataset creation - (1) AF-Next caption generation and LLM-based prompt augmentation, (2) MMAudio audio rendering, (3) retrieval of references from the 551K leakage-free pool. Middle: prior AC-V2A models train with a self-reference cropped from the GT audio. Bottom: ConRet trains under a retrieval-augmented contrastive forward where reference inputs can be either self-cropped or retrieved, and the two output trajectories are contrasted against each other.

Figure 2

Data pipeline. Per anchor: (1) an LLM expands the category text into 11 source/context-perturbed prompts (P0-P4c); (2) a pre-trained text-conditioned MMAudio renders each prompt; (3) multi-stage quality filtering keeps the well-synced subset (~700K). (4) the retrieval module pairs the surviving augmented clips against the leakage-free reference DB (~550K) under similarity-based filtering, producing the curated contrastive pair pools: Pool A (679,829 GTxAug pairs for T2A training) and Pool B (71,454 AugxAug pairs for V2A Stage 2 retrieval training).

Figure 3

Two contrastive training branches. Left: CONRET-T2A uses a 3-way forward pass per step - an Anchor (no reference) plus two reference branches (self-cropped or retrieved), with contrastive losses pulling the Anchor toward GT-ref and pushing GT-ref / Aug-ref apart on a denoiser feature. Right: CONRET-V2A uses a 2-way forward pass - the GT video acts as an implicit anchor, so only the push between the two reference branches is retained. Both branches share the same adapter and MSE on the flow target; only the forward count and contrast topology differ.
